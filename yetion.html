Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know before your Himalayan adventure

Fitness requirements vary by trek. Easy treks like Poon Hill are suitable for anyone with moderate fitness who can walk 4–6 hours per day. Challenging treks like Everest Base Camp require good cardiovascular fitness. We recommend starting training 2–3 months before your trek, focusing on cardio and hiking with a loaded backpack.
The best trekking seasons are Spring (March–May) and Autumn (September–November). Spring offers warmer temperatures and blooming rhododendrons, while Autumn provides the clearest skies and best mountain views. Winter treks (December–February) are possible at lower altitudes.
While not legally required for all routes, we strongly recommend trekking with a licensed guide for safety, navigation, cultural insights, and to support the local economy. Porters carry your heavy gear, allowing you to trek comfortably with just a daypack. Some restricted areas like Manaslu legally require a guide.
Altitude sickness (AMS) is a real concern above 2,500m. Our itineraries include proper acclimatization days, gradual altitude gains, and our guides are trained in recognizing and managing altitude-related illnesses. We carry oximeters and first aid kits, and have evacuation plans in place.
Our trek prices include: airport transfers, hotel accommodation in cities, all meals during the trek, an experienced English-speaking guide, porter service, all permits, and a first aid kit. Excluded items include international flights, Nepal visa, travel insurance, personal expenses, and tips.
Yes, comprehensive travel insurance covering emergency helicopter evacuation up to 6,000m is mandatory for all our treks. We recommend policies that include trip cancellation, medical expenses, and emergency evacuation. We can help recommend insurance providers specializing in adventure travel.
